Colombian Music

Guillermo Uribe Holguín

Date of birth: March 17, 1880

Date of death: June 26, 1971

Place of birth: Bogotá, Colombia

In 1891, Holguín entered la Academia Nacional de Música (Academy of National Music) to further his violin studies, and at 15 he became an instructor at the institution. In 1910, he returned from his travels and became the director of la Academia Nacional de Música renaming it Conservatorio Nacional de Música (National Conservatory of Music). He composed, played, and taught music until 1935.

His biggest contribution was the introduction of unique nationalist elements as stated in Colombia: A Concise Contemporary History, "... Guillermo Uribe Holguiń is regarded as the most influential at the turn of the twentieth century… Uribe Holguiń's work introduced unique nationalist elements in terms of melody and rhythm..." (LaRosa & Mejía, 212).

Luis Eduardo (Lucho) Bermúdez

Date of birth: January 25, 1912

Date of death: April 23, 1994

Place of birth: El Carmen de Bolívar, Bolívar, Colombia

Bermúdez started playing the piccolo at 5-years-old when his uncle José María Montes introduced him to the instrument. At the age of 9, he joined the Banda Militar and learned to play the bombardín, trombone, and clarinet, and began composing with teachers Mier and Noguera. By the time he was 21, he was the youngest and most prolific Colombian composer.

His biggest contribution was the introduction of historically "black" music to the mestizo and white population of Colombia. As stated in Colombia: A Concise Contemporary History, "... the Elvis Presley of Colombia, but he, like Elvis, made traditional black music “safe” and comfortable for middle-class mestizos and whites in the interior cities and throughout the Colombian nation” (LaRosa & Mejía, 212).

Top Hits

  1. "Colombia Tierra Querida"
  2. "Prende la Vela"
  3. "Tolú"


Carlos Alberto Vives Restrepo

Date of birth: August 7, 1961

Date of death: Still alive

Place of birth: Santa Marta, Magdalena, Colombia

At the age of 12, Vives moved to Bogotá and he encountered rock & roll for the first time and took part in the local scene as lead vocalist and guitarist. He has a marketing degree from Jorge Tadeo Lozano University. He took interest in acting while playing music on the side. He formed the band La Provencia and began performing a popular national style called vallenato. He gained extreme popularity and toured Colombia and other South American countries.

His biggest contribution was reintroduciung the genre of vallenatos as modern, which spread internationally. As stated in Colombia: A Concise Contemporary History, "...Carlos Vives from Santa Marta, made a recording of traditional vallenatos modified with a new, modern, upbeat tempo and performed by musicians trained in the vallenato tradition. His 1993 album, Clásicos de la Provincia, became an instant hit in Colombia, Latin America, and some parts of Europe and the United States" (LaRosa & Mejía, 212).

Top Hits

  1. "La Bicicleta"

  2. "Robarte Un Beso"

  3. "Fruta Fresca"






Shakira Isabel Mebarak Ripoll (Shakira)

Date of birth: February 2, 1977

Date of death: Still alive

Place of birth: Barranquilla, Colombia

At the age of 10, Shakira started writing songs and participating in talent competitions. In 1990, she landed an audition with Sony Corporation, and was signed to a record deal. Her first two albums, "Magia" and "Peligro" were moderately successful, so she took a break to film a Colombian telenovela "El Oasis."" She then resumed her career and started to focus on building her career in English, which led to multiple "Billboard" hits.

Her biggest contribution to Colombia was being able to go international with her music. In doing so, many people started to associate Colombia with her talents, as stated, "Shakira is a Colombian singer credited with being one of the biggest breakthrough Latin artists"("Shakira").

Top Hits

  1. "La Tortura"
  2. "Hips Don't Lie"
  3. "Whenever, Wherever"
